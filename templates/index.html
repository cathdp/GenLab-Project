Data Analytics In The Lab

We have entered a new age of information in medicine, one in which the scale and complexity of data, as well as the pace at which data are being generated, limits the ability of any individual to manually interpret it and draw relevant conclusions about the optimal course of therapy for a particular patient.

Laboratory Overview

Recent years have seen an expansion of resources available for data analytics across industries, including new, cost-effective tools for data transmission, storage, security, interoperability, computation, analysis, and visualization. Pathologists and clinical laboratories are uniquely positioned to apply advances in data analytics to improve patient care. Each of these advances is part of a larger, more general approach that has spawned the field of “Big Data,” and one that precision medicine is particularly well positioned to exploit. The field tends to be characterized by five “V’s”: volume, velocity, variety, veracity, and value.
